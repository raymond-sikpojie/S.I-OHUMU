About me

There are five things of great importance to me: young people, climate action, mental wellness, gender equity, and art. To serve those interests, I run a social enterprise, manage a community of young Nigerians, and produce social impact stories - using text, video, and sound. As a storyteller, I was most recently a part of the team that made the BBC documentary ‘Sex for Grades’. Its impact was widespread and has led to the reintroduction of the anti sexual harassment bill at the Nigerian senate. My social enterprise, Ubini, houses the community, #SpaceBenin, which promotes self expression, mental wellness and collaboration among young creative Nigerians. We have hosted six editions and have over 300 members. I am a member of the National Youth Climate Innovation Hub, a writer for the Venice Biennale catalogue, and have worked for the BBC, the British Council, and the Edo State Government. So now you know everything about me. Except for this: when I'm not working and even sometimes when I am, I am a ray of light and a burst of sunshine. If you work with me, you’ll be doing work that matters, with a smile on your face and joy in your heart.
